## Center Overview Section
**Heading:** More Than a Building - A Community Home
**Icon:** `fa-home`

The WOW Heritage Center operates as both a neighborhood gathering place and a specialized hub for community storytelling work. We believe physical spaces matter—they provide stability, accessibility, and belonging that strengthen community connections and amplify community voices.

**Core Functions:**
- **Community Gathering Space:** Free, accessible venue for neighborhood meetings, celebrations, and informal connection
- **Storytelling Production Hub:** Professional-grade equipment and technical support for community story creation
- **Learning & Workshop Center:** Regular programming in digital literacy, creative skills, and community engagement
- **Community Archive & Resource Center:** Physical and digital archives accessible to community members and researchers

**Operating Principles:**
- **Community Control:** Programming and space use guided by community priorities and feedback
- **Radical Accessibility:** Physical, economic, and cultural accessibility for all community members
- **Intergenerational Design:** Spaces that welcome and serve community members of all ages
- **Cultural Responsiveness:** Environment that reflects and honors diverse community cultures and traditions

## Community Archive & Preservation Section
**Heading:** Preserving Our Stories for Future Generations
**Icon:** `fa-archive`

### Archive Mission:
The WOW Heritage Center Archive preserves community stories, documents, and artifacts that might otherwise be lost, ensuring that community knowledge and perspective remain accessible to current and future residents.

### Community Contributions:
**Personal & Family Materials:** Family photos, documents, letters, and artifacts that tell individual and family stories
**Business & Organization Records:** Documentation of local businesses, churches, and community organizations over time
**Neighborhood Development:** Photos and documents showing physical and social changes in the community
**Cultural & Social Life:** Materials documenting celebrations, traditions, social networks, and community events
**Activism & Organizing:** Records of community organizing, advocacy, and social justice work led by residents

### Archive Services:

**Preservation Support:**
- Document and photo preservation consultation
- Basic digitization services for community contributors  
- Archival storage and organization for contributed materials
- Preparation guidance for donating materials to larger archives

**Research Assistance:**
- Support for community members researching family or neighborhood history
- Connection to broader historical resources and archives
- Assistance with genealogy and family history research
- Academic research support for students and scholars studying the community

**Community Education:**
- Workshops on document preservation and family history research
- Training in oral history methods and community story collection
- Digital literacy support for accessing online archives and resources
- Community history walks and educational programming

### Access & Usage:
**Open Research Hours:** Tuesday 2PM-6PM, Saturday 10AM-2PM
**Appointment Access:** Available for researchers and community members needing focused time
**Digital Access:** Online catalog and selected digital materials available 24/7
**Community Events:** Regular programming highlighting archive materials and community history

## Space Rental & Community Use Section
**Heading:** Your Community Space - Use It!
**Icon:** `fa-key`

### Community Meeting & Event Space:
The WOW Heritage Center prioritizes community use and keeps rental costs low to ensure accessibility for neighborhood organizations and residents.

### Rental Options:

**Community Organization Meetings:**
- **Rate:** Free for registered community organizations
- **Includes:** Basic room setup, AV equipment, kitchen access for refreshments
- **Capacity:** Main room (75 people), workshop rooms (15 people each)

**Private Community Events:**
- **Rate:** $50-150 sliding scale based on event size and renter's ability to pay
- **Includes:** Space rental, basic setup, kitchen access, cleanup support
- **Examples:** Birthday parties, family reunions, cultural celebrations, memorial services

**Nonprofit & Educational Events:**
- **Rate:** $25-100 sliding scale
- **Includes:** Space, AV support, promotional support through E4 Youth networks
- **Examples:** Community workshops, advocacy meetings, educational presentations

**Small Business & Professional Use:**
- **Rate:** Market rate ($150-300) with portion supporting community programming
- **Includes:** Professional space rental with full AV and hospitality support
- **Examples:** Professional development workshops, small conferences, training sessions

### Rental Process:
1. **Space Inquiry:** Contact Heritage Center to discuss event needs and date availability
2. **Community Review:** All events reviewed to ensure alignment with community priorities and center mission
3. **Event Planning:** Collaborative planning to maximize community benefit and event success
4. **Event Support:** Staff and volunteer support available for event logistics and technical needs
5. **Community Integration:** Opportunities to connect events with broader community programming and participation

### Rental Policies:
**Community Priority:** Community organizations and residents receive priority for space booking
**Sliding Scale:** All rates negotiable based on ability to pay - no one turned away for financial reasons
**Community Benefit:** All events should provide some benefit to broader community, not just private benefit
**Cultural Respect:** All events must respect the diversity and values of the community served by the Heritage Center

**CTA:** Reserve Community Space
