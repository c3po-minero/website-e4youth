## For Young People: Your Creative Journey Starts Here Section
**Heading:** Programs Designed for Your Growth and Goals
**Icon:** `fa-rocket`

### Ages 12-15: Digital Storytelling Program (DSP)
**Icon:** `fa-play-circle`
**What You'll Do:** Learn video production, interviewing, digital editing, and storytelling fundamentals while exploring your community and building creative confidence.

**Time Commitment:** 2 hours weekly for 8-week sessions, plus optional community events
**What You'll Gain:** Technical skills, creative portfolio pieces, presentation experience, and readiness for advanced programming
**Next Steps:** Advance to Get Creative! or continue with advanced DSP workshops

**How to Join:**
- Attend a free intro workshop (held monthly)
- Complete online application with parent/guardian involvement
- Participate in informal interview/meet-and-greet session

**Application Deadline:** Rolling admission, with new cohorts starting every quarter
**Cost:** Free for all participants

### Ages 16-18: Get Creative!
**Icon:** `fa-palette`
**What You'll Do:** Develop professional creative and technology skills while exploring career pathways, building portfolios, and connecting with industry mentors.

**Time Commitment:** 3 hours weekly for 12-week program, plus networking events and showcase participation
**What You'll Gain:** Professional portfolio, industry connections, college preparation support, internship opportunities
**Next Steps:** College applications with enhanced portfolios, internship placements, or advancement to HIP programming

**Prerequisites:** Completion of DSP or equivalent creative/digital experience
**Application Process:** Portfolio review, written application, and group interview
**Application Deadline:** Fall and spring cohorts, applications due 6 weeks before start date

### Ages 18-25: Heritage & Innovation Pathways (HIP)
**Icon:** `fa-bridge-circle-check`
**What You'll Do:** Conduct community research, create professional documentaries, develop public presentations, and build advanced creative and professional skills.

**Time Commitment:** 4-6 hours weekly for 16-week program, including independent project work
**What You'll Gain:** Advanced portfolio, professional network, community leadership experience, clear pathways to employment or continued education
**Outcomes:** Paid internship placement, job referrals, graduate school preparation, or community leadership roles

**Prerequisites:** High school graduation or equivalent, plus interview process
**Application Process:** Detailed application, portfolio submission, community project proposal, and panel interview
**Application Periods:** Annual cohort, applications open in spring for fall start

### All Ages: Community Workshops & Events
**Icon:** `fa-calendar-star`
**What's Available:** Monthly skill-building workshops, quarterly showcase events, annual community celebration, and special collaborative projects.

**Examples of Recent Workshops:**
- **Oral History Basics:** Learn ethical interview techniques and community storytelling approaches
- **Digital Editing Fundamentals:** Hands-on training with professional video and audio editing software
- **Professional Presentation Skills:** Practice public speaking and portfolio presentation with supportive audience
- **Community Research Methods:** Develop investigation and documentation skills for historical and cultural projects

**How to Participate:** 
- Sign up for monthly newsletter to receive workshop announcements
- Follow social media for real-time updates and registration links
- Attend E4 Live events to learn about upcoming opportunities

**Cost:** Free for community members, small suggested donation for non-Austin residents

**CTA:** Apply for Programs

## For Families: Supporting Your Young Person's Growth Section
**Heading:** Family Engagement That Honors Community Knowledge
**Icon:** `fa-home-heart`

E4 Youth recognizes that families are young people's first community and most important support system. Our family engagement goes beyond permission slips and progress reports to create meaningful opportunities for families to contribute community knowledge, develop their own skills, and strengthen relationships across generations.

### Family Orientation and Ongoing Communication
**What This Includes:**
- Comprehensive program overview and expectation setting
- Regular communication about participant progress and upcoming opportunities  
- Family input on programming adjustments and community feedback
- Access to staff for questions, concerns, or celebration of achievements

**Time Commitment:** Monthly family meetings (in-person or virtual) plus quarterly celebration events
**Family Benefits:** Clear communication, program transparency, connection with other families, understanding of participant's growth

### Intergenerational Programming
**What This Looks Like:**
- **Family storytelling workshops** where multiple generations share knowledge and learn digital skills together
- **Community oral history projects** that honor elder knowledge while developing young people's research skills
- **Cultural preservation initiatives** that document family and community traditions using professional digital tools
- **Showcase events** where families present collaborative work alongside individual participant projects

**Recent Intergenerational Projects:**
- **"Recipes and Remembrance":** Families documented traditional cooking methods and family food histories through video and digital storytelling
- **"Neighborhood Knowledge":** Multi-generational teams researched and documented community changes over time
- **"Cultural Celebration Archive":** Families collaborated to create digital preservation of community cultural traditions and celebrations

### Family Professional Development
**Skills Development Opportunities:**
- **Technology training** in digital storytelling tools so families can continue creative work at home
- **Communication skills** for supporting young people's educational and professional development
- **Community engagement** training for families interested in local leadership and advocacy roles
- **Cultural documentation** approaches for preserving family and community knowledge

**Example Family Outcomes:**
- Parents developing their own creative practices and professional portfolios
- Grandparents becoming community oral history facilitators
- Siblings engaging in peer mentorship and community leadership
- Extended families creating lasting documentation of cultural knowledge and family heritage

### Family Leadership and Decision-Making
**How Families Shape Programming:**
- **Family advisory committee** that provides input on program development and community priorities
- **Evaluation participation** through regular feedback sessions and program improvement suggestions  
- **Community event planning** where families help design celebrations, showcases, and community engagement
- **Policy advocacy** support for families interested in engaging with educational and youth development policy conversations
