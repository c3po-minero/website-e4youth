## Program Vision Section
**Heading:** Where Community Need Meets Professional Development
**Icon:** `fa-handshake`

HIP operates on the principle that the most meaningful learning happens when individual skill development serves authentic community needs. Participants work on real projects with real impact while developing advanced professional competencies and career connections.

**Core Approach:**
- **Community-Centered Projects:** Every HIP project addresses community-identified priorities and needs
- **Professional-Level Work:** Participants produce work that meets professional standards and deadlines
- **Mentorship & Networks:** Extensive connection with established professionals across creative and civic sectors
- **Leadership Development:** Participants lead projects, mentor younger students, and represent E4 Youth in professional settings

**Who This Serves:**
- **Primary Participants:** College students and emerging adults ages 18-25
- **Academic Backgrounds:** All majors welcome, with particular focus on creative, communication, social justice, and public service fields
- **Experience Level:** Previous creative or community engagement experience preferred but not required
- **Commitment Level:** Participants ready for professional-level responsibility and community engagement

## Program Structure & Expectations Section
**Heading:** Year-Long Commitment, Lasting Impact
**Icon:** `fa-calendar`

### Program Timeline:
**Duration:** 12-month commitment (September - August)
**Time Investment:** 15-20 hours per week including project work, mentorship, and community engagement
**Structure:** Combination of independent project work, group sessions, and community events

### Program Components:

**Individual Project Development (50% of time):**
- Major community-engaged project developed over full program year
- Regular mentor meetings and progress consultations
- Community partner collaboration and relationship management
- Professional presentation and public sharing of completed work

**Collaborative Learning (25% of time):**
- Weekly cohort meetings for skill-building and peer support
- Monthly workshops with professional guest experts
- Quarterly intensive working sessions and retreats
- Cross-project collaboration and mutual support

**Community Engagement (25% of time):**
- Regular participation in community events and meetings
- Mentorship of participants in DSP and Get Creative! programs
- Public presentations and community education activities
- E4 Youth program support and organizational contribution

### Program Expectations:

**Professional Standards:** All work meets professional quality and deadline requirements
**Community Accountability:** Participants are accountable to community partners and project stakeholders
**Peer Support:** Active participation in collaborative learning and mutual mentorship
**Organizational Contribution:** Support for E4 Youth's mission and community presence

## Application & Selection Process Section
**Heading:** Ready for Advanced Community-Engaged Learning?
**Icon:** `fa-clipboard-check`

### Application Requirements:

**Project Proposal:** 500-word proposal for community-engaged project you'd like to develop during HIP
**Portfolio & Resume:** Examples of previous creative work, community engagement, or academic projects
**Academic Transcript:** College-level coursework (community college or 4-year institution)
**References:** Two references from professors, employers, or community partners
**Personal Statement:** 750-word essay about your interest in community-engaged creative work

### Selection Process:

**Application Review:** Holistic evaluation of proposal quality, experience, and program fit
**Interview Process:** 45-minute conversation with program staff and community partners
**Community Engagement:** Attendance at community event or project presentation
**Reference Checks:** Conversation with provided references about applicant readiness

### Selection Criteria:

**Community Commitment:** Demonstrated interest in community-engaged work and social justice
**Professional Readiness:** Ability to meet professional standards and work independently
**Collaborative Skills:** Experience working effectively with diverse teams and partners
**Growth Mindset:** Willingness to learn from community members and adapt projects based on feedback
**Cultural Humility:** Understanding of power dynamics and commitment to equitable community engagement

### Application Timeline:

**Annual Cohort (12-month commitment):**
- Applications Open: April 1st
- Application Deadline: June 1st
- Interviews: June 10-25th
- Community Engagement Events: June 15-30th
- Acceptance Notifications: July 1st
- Program Orientation: August 15-20th
- Program Begins: September 1st

*Note: HIP accepts one cohort per year with intensive year-long commitment. Mid-year entry not available.*
