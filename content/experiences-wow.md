## Platform Overview Section
**Heading:** Stories Deserve Public Spaces
**Icon:** `fa-globe`

What Once Was (WOW) operates on the belief that community stories—especially those from young people and historically marginalized voices—deserve prominent, accessible platforms for sharing and preservation.

**Core Mission:**
- **Amplify Marginalized Voices:** Center stories that are often excluded from official histories and public memory
- **Create Dialogue Opportunities:** Design experiences that bring people together across differences of age, race, class, and experience
- **Preserve Community Knowledge:** Document and share stories that might otherwise be lost to time or displacement
- **Strengthen Community Identity:** Help neighborhoods understand their own histories and envision their futures

**Platform Components:**
- **Digital Exhibitions:** Online galleries showcasing multimedia storytelling projects
- **Physical Installations:** Location-based experiences that bring stories into public spaces
- **Community Events:** Gatherings that celebrate stories and create opportunities for dialogue
- **Archive & Research:** Preservation systems that maintain community stories for future access

## Current WOW Experiences Section
**Heading:** Stories You Can Engage With Right Now
**Icon:** `fa-map-location-dot`

### Walk With History: The Jacob Fontaine AR Tour
**Location:** East Austin neighborhoods
**Format:** Self-guided augmented reality experience
**Technology:** Mobile app with GPS-triggered content
**Community Partnership:** East Austin historical preservation groups

Experience Black Austin history through the eyes of community members who lived it. This location-based AR tour brings historical narratives directly into the spaces where they happened, featuring stories collected and presented by youth participants working with community elders.

**Tour Highlights:**
- Personal stories from longtime residents about neighborhood changes
- Historical context for significant locations and landmarks
- Youth-created multimedia content bringing archival materials to life
- Community-guided interpretation that centers resident perspectives

**CTA:** Download AR Tour App

### Voices of Resilience Digital Exhibition
**Location:** Online platform with periodic pop-up installations
**Format:** Multimedia storytelling gallery
**Featured:** Stories of community strength during challenging times
**Community Partnership:** Local mutual aid organizations

This ongoing digital exhibition features stories of how community members have supported each other through everything from economic challenges to natural disasters, highlighting the often-invisible networks that keep neighborhoods strong.

**Exhibition Sections:**
- "Mutual Aid Networks" - Stories of community support systems
- "Creative Survival" - How people maintain joy and creativity during hardship  
- "Intergenerational Wisdom" - Knowledge passed down through families and communities
- "Future Visions" - Community members' hopes and plans for neighborhood development

**CTA:** Visit Digital Exhibition

### Memory Keepers Community Archive
**Location:** WOW Heritage Center and online
**Format:** Community-contributed archive and storytelling collection
**Focus:** Preserving everyday community life and significant moments
**Community Partnership:** Neighborhood associations and cultural organizations

This collaborative archive invites community members to contribute their own stories, photos, documents, and memories, creating a comprehensive record of community life that centers resident voices and experiences.

**Archive Collections:**
- Family histories and immigration stories
- Business and economic development over time
- Cultural traditions and community celebrations
- Environmental and neighborhood changes
- Community organizing and social justice work

## Story Development Process Section
**Heading:** From Personal Experience to Public Impact
**Icon:** `fa-route`

### Community Engagement Phase:
**Story Collection:** Working with community members to identify stories that matter to them
**Relationship Building:** Developing trust and ongoing partnerships with story contributors
**Context Development:** Understanding the broader historical and social context for individual stories
**Permission & Ethics:** Ensuring contributors have control over how their stories are shared

### Creative Development Phase:
**Multimedia Production:** Creating compelling presentations using video, audio, photography, and interactive elements
**Youth Leadership:** E4 Youth participants lead creative development while working closely with story contributors
**Community Review:** Regular check-ins with contributors to ensure accurate and respectful representation
**Platform Design:** Developing appropriate ways to share stories based on contributor preferences and community needs

### Public Engagement Phase:
**Platform Launch:** Releasing stories through appropriate WOW platforms and community events
**Community Celebration:** Events that honor story contributors and celebrate community knowledge
**Ongoing Dialogue:** Creating opportunities for community response and continued conversation
**Archive Integration:** Ensuring stories are preserved and accessible for future community use

### Impact & Evaluation Phase:
**Community Feedback:** Regular assessment of how well WOW experiences serve community needs
**Story Impact:** Tracking how stories influence community conversations and development
**Platform Development:** Using community input to improve and expand WOW offerings
**Relationship Maintenance:** Ongoing partnership with story contributors and community organizations
