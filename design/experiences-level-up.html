Level Up: Where Skills Meet Strategy

E4 Level Up is an advanced workshop and professional development series for college-aged, early-career, and established creatives. These intensive learning experiences combine cutting-edge skills training with strategic thinking, professional portfolio development, and industry mentorship.

Explore Level Up Workshops Partner on Level Up

Beyond Basic Training: Strategic Creative Development

Most professional development programs treat creative skills and business strategy as separate domains. E4 Level Up recognizes that today's creative professionals must be both technically excellent and strategically minded—able to execute exceptional work while understanding its impact, market context, and professional applications.

Applied Learning

Every workshop centers real projects and challenges rather than theoretical concepts

Industry Integration

Working professionals co-facilitate workshops, bringing current industry practices and standards

Portfolio Development

Participants leave with tangible work samples and professional documentation

Strategic Thinking

Technical skills training integrates business strategy, client relations, and career pathway planning

Connect with Professional Community

Designed for Emerging and Established Creative Professionals

College-Aged Participants (18-22)

Focus: Building professional-caliber portfolios and industry readiness
Background: Current students in creative programs seeking practical, industry-relevant skill development
Goals: Internship preparation, portfolio refinement, professional network development

Early-Career Creatives (22-30)

Focus: Advanced skill development and career advancement strategy
Background: 1-5 years of professional experience seeking specialization or advancement
Goals: Skill diversification, leadership development, client relationship building

Established Professionals (30+)

Focus: Industry innovation and community mentorship leadership
Background: Experienced professionals seeking new techniques or community engagement
Goals: Staying current with emerging tools, developing teaching/mentorship skills

Career Changers (All Ages)

Focus: Translating existing skills into creative industries
Background: Professionals from other fields seeking creative career transition
Goals: Portfolio development, industry knowledge, professional network building

Specialized Learning Tracks for Diverse Creative Goals

Digital Storytelling Mastery

Duration: 6-week intensive series
Schedule: Weekly 4-hour Saturday sessions
Class Size: Maximum 12 participants
Prerequisites: Basic video editing and storytelling experience

Core Components:

  • Advanced narrative structure and audience analysis
  • Professional documentary production techniques
  • Multi-platform content strategy and distribution
  • Client communication and project management

Industry Tools: Adobe Premiere Pro, After Effects, Audition, DaVinci Resolve

Register for Digital Storytelling Track

Creative Technology Integration

Duration: 8-week hybrid learning experience
Schedule: Bi-weekly in-person labs plus online collaboration
Class Size: Maximum 10 participants
Prerequisites: Comfort with creative software and basic coding concepts

Core Components:

  • AR/VR storytelling and experience design
  • Interactive media development (web, mobile, installation)
  • Creative coding for multimedia projects
  • User experience design for storytelling platforms

Industry Tools: Unity, TouchDesigner, p5.js, Figma, Arduino/Raspberry Pi

Explore Creative Technology Track

Professional Communication & Client Relations

Duration: 4-week intensive workshop series
Schedule: Weekly 3-hour evening sessions
Class Size: Maximum 15 participants
Prerequisites: At least 1 year of creative work experience

Core Components:

  • Client discovery and project scoping techniques
  • Professional presentation and pitch development
  • Contract negotiation and project management
  • Creative feedback integration and revision processes

Final Deliverable: Complete client pitch package and business development materials

Join Communication Skills Track

Community-Centered Research Methods

Duration: 10-week research and development program
Schedule: Weekly 2.5-hour sessions plus fieldwork
Class Size: Maximum 8 participants
Prerequisites: Interest in community engagement and social impact work

Core Components:

  • Ethical community engagement and participatory research methods
  • Oral history collection and archive development
  • Community asset mapping and stakeholder analysis
  • Grant writing and program evaluation for creative projects

Final Deliverable: Complete community research project with public presentation

Apply for Research Methods Program

Ready to Level Up Your Creative Career?

Application Process

  1. Workshop Selection: Review workshop descriptions and select the track that best matches your professional goals
  2. Application Submission: Submit portfolio samples, professional goals statement, and prerequisite verification
  3. Interview Process: Brief conversation with workshop facilitators to ensure program fit and discuss expectations
  4. Cohort Confirmation: Receive acceptance notification with specific workshop details and preparation materials

Investment & Financial Aid

Workshop fees range from $500-1,200 depending on duration and intensity. Financial aid and payment plans available for all workshops. No one turned away for financial reasons.

What You'll Need

  • Portfolio Samples: 3-5 examples of your current creative work
  • Goals Statement: Brief description of your professional development objectives
  • Prerequisites: Verification of required skills and experience for your chosen track
  • Time Commitment: Availability for all workshop sessions and project work

Application Deadlines

Fall Workshops: Applications due August 15
Spring Workshops: Applications due January 15
Summer Intensives: Applications due May 1
