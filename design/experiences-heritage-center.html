A Physical Home for Community Stories

The WOW Heritage Center serves as E4 Youth's community hub—a welcoming space where neighbors gather to share stories, learn new skills, access resources, and build the connections that strengthen community life.

Visit the Center Reserve Space

More Than a Building - A Community Home

The WOW Heritage Center operates as both a neighborhood gathering place and a specialized hub for community storytelling work. We believe physical spaces matter—they provide stability, accessibility, and belonging that strengthen community connections and amplify community voices.

Community Gathering Space

Free, accessible venue for neighborhood meetings, celebrations, and informal connection

Storytelling Production Hub

Professional-grade equipment and technical support for community story creation

Learning & Workshop Center

Regular programming in digital literacy, creative skills, and community engagement

Community Archive & Resource Center

Physical and digital archives accessible to community members and researchers

Operating Principles

Community Control

Programming and space use guided by community priorities and feedback

Radical Accessibility

Physical, economic, and cultural accessibility for all community members

Intergenerational Design

Spaces that welcome and serve community members of all ages

Cultural Responsiveness

Environment that reflects and honors diverse community cultures and traditions

Designed for Community, Built for Stories

Main Community Room

Capacity: 75 people for events, 40 for workshops
Features: Flexible seating, projection system, sound amplification, live-streaming capability
Accessibility: Fully ADA-accessible with assistive listening devices

Digital Storytelling Lab

Capacity: 15 workstations
Equipment: Professional cameras, audio recording, editing computers, lighting
Software: Adobe Creative Suite, video editing, audio production tools

Community Archive & Research Area

Features: Climate-controlled storage, digitization equipment, research tables
Access: Open research hours with volunteer assistance
Services: Document preservation, digitization support

Kitchen & Hospitality Space

Features: Full kitchen facilities, serving areas, refrigeration
Use: Event catering, cooking workshops, community meals
Programming: Regular cooking classes and cultural food exchanges

Flexible Workshop Rooms

Configuration: Two rooms that can be combined or separate
Capacity: 12-15 people each
Features: Moveable furniture, wall displays, project storage

Outdoor Community Space

Features: Covered pavilion, garden areas, performance space, children's play area
Programming: Outdoor events, gardening workshops, performances
Accessibility: Paved pathways, accessible seating, shade structures

Regular Programming That Builds Community

Weekly Programming

  • Monday Digital Literacy Drop-In (10AM-12PM)
    Open computer lab with volunteer support for digital skills and online services access
  • Tuesday Community Archive Hours (2PM-6PM)
    Supported time for community members to contribute to and research community archive
  • Wednesday Family Story Time (6PM-7:30PM)
    Intergenerational storytelling combining traditional and digital story creation
  • Thursday Community Forum (7PM-8:30PM)
    Regular meetings addressing neighborhood issues and resident-led planning
  • Friday Creative Labs (4PM-7PM)
    Open creative time with digital storytelling equipment and peer collaboration
  • Saturday Community Workshops
    Monthly themed workshops addressing community-identified interests and needs
  • Sunday Community Connection (3PM-5PM)
    Weekly informal gathering with refreshments and community updates

Monthly Special Programming

  • First Friday Community Showcase
  • Third Saturday Skill Shares
  • Last Sunday Community Planning

Your Community Space - Use It!

The WOW Heritage Center prioritizes community use and keeps rental costs low to ensure accessibility for neighborhood organizations and residents.

Community Organization Meetings

Rate: Free for registered community organizations
Includes: Basic room setup, AV equipment, kitchen access
Capacity: Main room (75 people), workshop rooms (15 people each)

Private Community Events

Rate: $50-150 sliding scale
Includes: Space rental, basic setup, kitchen access, cleanup support
Examples: Birthday parties, family reunions, cultural celebrations

Nonprofit & Educational Events

Rate: $25-100 sliding scale
Includes: Space, AV support, promotional support
Examples: Community workshops, advocacy meetings, educational presentations

Small Business & Professional Use

Rate: Market rate ($150-300)
Includes: Professional space rental with full AV and hospitality support
Examples: Professional development, small conferences, training sessions

Rental Policies

Community Priority: Organizations and residents receive priority

Sliding Scale: All rates negotiable - no one turned away for financial reasons

Community Benefit: All events should provide some community benefit

Cultural Respect: All events must respect community diversity and values

Designed for Everyone in Our Community

Physical Accessibility

Full ADA compliance including ramps, accessible restrooms, adjustable-height workspaces, assistive technology, hearing loops, and accessible transportation options

Economic Accessibility

All regular programming offered at no cost, sliding scale for all services, free access to computers and internet, regular free community meals

Language & Cultural Accessibility

Multilingual support, interpretation and translation services, cultural responsiveness in programming, respect for diverse religious practices

Family & Childcare Support

Family-friendly events, childcare assistance for meetings and workshops, multi-generational programming, family resource connections
